# Riddles and Revelations
---

## Summary

**Recording Note:** There is missing recording from either the latter half of Session 18 or the first half of this session. The riverside shack quest (where [[Party Members/Elvva Beltavar\|Elvva Beltavar]] was poisoned by a cactus plant) was not recorded and information relies on handwritten notes from players.

The party was summoned to a royal feast at seven o'clock, giving them time for a short rest beforehand. At the feast, all tournament competitors were present except [[NPCs/Ser Balthazar Thornwood\|Ser Balthazar Thornwood]] (deceased) and [[Party Members/Elvva Beltavar\|Elvva Beltavar]] (recovering from injuries). The [[NPCs/Lord Commander\|Lord Commander]] attended, having been released for fighting against the attackers. During the feast, all competitors - and notably [[NPCs/Crown Princess Eilrora Everglade\|Crown Princess Eilrora Everglade]] herself - were drugged and fell unconscious. They awakened individually in identical small rooms for "The Tournament of Wits and Wisdom," a puzzle-based elimination challenge announced by [[NPCs/Eldrin Oakenhart\|Eldrin Oakenhart]]'s magically amplified voice. Each competitor faced a series of five increasingly difficult riddles in separate, isolated rooms. Wrong answers inflicted elemental damage: cold (Room 1), fire (Room 2), lightning (Room 3), and earth (Room 4). The challenge tested both intelligence and constitution, as accumulated damage could knock competitors out. Success depended on solving riddles quickly whilst managing damage from incorrect guesses. **Only five competitors total** (out of all tournament participants) completed all five rooms - many were knocked out. An unknown NPC completed first overall, followed by [[Party Members/Elik'r Stormswallow\|Elik'r Stormswallow]] (second overall, first among party members), then [[NPCs/Co'narr 'Banewatcher' Hyborian\|Co'narr 'Banewatcher' Hyborian]] (third overall), then [[Party Members/ToooAh\|ToooAh]] (fourth overall, second among party members), and finally [[Party Members/Lady Odonia of Galantri\|Lady Odonia of Galantri]] (fifth overall, third among party members). [[Party Members/Ser Tessell Highfrost\|Ser Tessell Highfrost]] was knocked out and did not complete.

### The Tournament of Wits and Wisdom

**The Awakening:**

- Each competitor woke in an **identical but separate small room** (individual instances)
- **No communication possible between competitors**
- Each person completely isolated
- No knowledge of how much time had passed
- Eleven walls of each room lit up in blue (magical illumination)
- Lord of the Rings-style magical effect spreading around walls

**[[NPCs/Eldrin Oakenhart\|Eldrin Oakenhart]]'s Announcement:**

- Voice boomed throughout all rooms simultaneously via spell
- Same spell all party members had used before (recognisable)
- Announced: "The Tournament of Wits and Wisdom"
- Explained rules and format
- Each competitor heard the same announcement in their separate room
- Magical amplification made voice fill entire space

**The Rules:**

- Five levels/rooms to complete **individually**
- Each room contains a riddle on the door
- Correct answer opens door to next room **for that competitor only**
- Wrong answers inflict damage and status effects **to that individual**
- **Two ways to win:**
    1. Complete all five levels successfully
    2. Fastest completion time among those who succeed
- **Competitors completely isolated** - cannot help each other at all
- Performance affects tournament rankings
- Each person progresses at their own pace

**Meta-Game Mechanics:**

- Players used mobile phones to message DM privately
- Answers submitted via direct message only
- **No shouting out answers** (competing against each other, in separate rooms)
- Time taken = order of message receipts
- **When one player solved their riddle, only that player progressed**
- Other players continued working on their own riddles in their own rooms
- DM had pre-rolled results for all NPCs
- 100 different riddles prepared in advance
- **Each competitor received different riddles** (rolled randomly via dice)

**Damage Types by Room:**

- **Room 1:** Blue door - Cold damage (5 points per wrong answer)
- **Room 2:** Red door - Fire damage (5 points per wrong answer)
- **Room 3:** Static/yellow - Lightning damage (5 points per wrong answer)
- **Room 4:** Brown/orange - Earth/crushing damage (5 points per wrong answer)
- **Room 5:** Unknown damage type (final challenge)

### Individual Riddle Progression

**Important Note:** Each party member was in their **own separate challenge instance**. When one person solved a riddle and moved to the next room, the others remained in whatever room they were currently in, working on their own riddles. Progress was completely individual.

## Memorable Moments

### The Poison in the Ice

**Full Description:** [[Party Members/Ser Tessell Highfrost\|Ser Tessell Highfrost]] received his first riddle: "Two men drink poisoned iced tea. One man drinks it fast and lives. The other man drinks it slow and dies. How is this possible?"

The room glowed blue around him as he pondered. He submitted several wrong answers, each time causing the temperature to plummet further. Ice spread across the walls and floor. With each incorrect guess, he took 5 points of cold damage, shivering violently.

"Come on, think!" he muttered to himself, alone in the frigid room.

More wrong answers. More damage. The cold was becoming unbearable. Finally, after significant struggle and accumulated damage, the realisation hit him: **the poison was in the ice cubes**. The man who drank slowly gave the ice time to melt and release the poison. The fast drinker finished before the poison could dissolve.

When he finally submitted the correct answer, the door glowed and opened, but [[Party Members/Ser Tessell Highfrost\|Ser Tessell Highfrost]] was already significantly weakened. This was only the first room.

**Significance:** Set the tone for [[Party Members/Ser Tessell Highfrost\|Ser Tessell Highfrost]]'s entire challenge - he would struggle with almost every riddle, accumulating heavy damage throughout.

---

### ToooAh and the Village Riddle

**Full Description:** [[Party Members/ToooAh\|ToooAh]] entered Room 2 and faced the red door's riddle: "As I was going to the village I met a man with seven wives. Each wife had seven kids. Each kid has seven cats, each cat had seven kittens. How many were going to the village?"

The bird-monk immediately began calculating: "Seven wives timesseven kids is forty-nine... times seven cats... times seven kittens..."

The numbers grew astronomical. Hundreds? Thousands?

He submitted a large number. The room erupted in flames - 5 fire damage. Wrong answer.

[[Party Members/ToooAh\|ToooAh]] recalculated, thinking he'd made a mathematical error. Another wrong answer. Another blast of heat and 5 more damage.

Then it hit him - this was a trick question. "**I** was going to the village. Just **one person**. Everyone else was coming **from** the village!"

He submitted "One" and the door opened. The flames subsided.

"Clever," [[Party Members/ToooAh\|ToooAh]] muttered, a bit annoyed at falling for the misdirection but impressed by the riddle's elegance.

**Significance:** Classic example of the challenge testing lateral thinking rather than mathematical ability. The riddle punished overthinking.

### The Carriage Sound Riddle

**Full Description:** [[Party Members/Ser Tessell Highfrost\|Ser Tessell Highfrost]], already battered and bruised from previous rooms, limped into Room 3. The yellow door crackled with static electricity. His riddle appeared: "What goes with a carriage, comes with a carriage, is of no use to a carriage and yet the carriage cannot go without it?"

"Wheels?" Wrong. Lightning zapped him for 5 damage.

"Horse?" Wrong. Another shock.

"Driver?" Wrong. Another 5 damage.

The DM noted: "This is the riddle my work colleagues couldn't solve either."

[[Party Members/Ser Tessell Highfrost\|Ser Tessell Highfrost]] tried variation after variation. "Reins?" "Axle?" "Wood?" "Metal?" Each answer brought another painful electric shock. His HP was dropping dangerously low.

Other players, having already solved their own Room 3 riddles, could only listen as [[Party Members/Ser Tessell Highfrost\|Ser Tessell Highfrost]] continued to struggle. They couldn't help - the rules forbade it.

"The wording is close," the DM offered as a hint. "You're on the right track with some attempts."

More wrong answers. More lightning. [[Party Members/Ser Tessell Highfrost\|Ser Tessell Highfrost]]'s character was becoming seriously wounded.

Finally, after what felt like an eternity and numerous shocks: **"The sound of a carriage!"** Or simply "noise" or "sound."

The door opened. [[Party Members/Ser Tessell Highfrost\|Ser Tessell Highfrost]] stumbled through, barely conscious, dreading whatever Room 4 would bring.

**Significance:** This riddle nearly eliminated [[Party Members/Ser Tessell Highfrost\|Ser Tessell Highfrost]] from the competition. The DM's note that professional colleagues also failed it highlighted its genuine difficulty. It showcased how the challenge could be brutally unforgiving.

### The Failed Drinking Deception

**Full Description:** [[Party Members/Elik'r Stormswallow\|Elik'r Stormswallow]] returned to the feast, exhausted but victorious. He was the first party member back, though one unknown NPC had completed before him.

The feast hall was still relatively quiet. Servants were clearing away some of the unconscious competitors or tending to those who remained in the challenge. The table before [[Party Members/Elik'r Stormswallow\|Elik'r Stormswallow]] held several pristine, untouched tankards of ale.

Then the air shimmered and [[NPCs/Co'narr 'Banewatcher' Hyborian\|Co'narr 'Banewatcher' Hyborian]] materialised back at his seat, looking pleased with himself.

[[Party Members/Elik'r Stormswallow\|Elik'r Stormswallow]] saw his opportunity. He gestured dramatically at the tankards: "Co'narr! You've got some serious catching up to do, mate! I've already finished all of these!"

**[Rolled Natural 1 on Deception]**

[[NPCs/Co'narr 'Banewatcher' Hyborian\|Co'narr 'Banewatcher' Hyborian]] looked at [[Party Members/Elik'r Stormswallow\|Elik'r Stormswallow]].

Then at the tankards - completely full, foam still fresh, not a single drop taken.

Then back at [[Party Members/Elik'r Stormswallow\|Elik'r Stormswallow]].

The massive Goliath's face split into an enormous grin. "You haven't touched a **single one**, have you lad?"

[[Party Members/Elik'r Stormswallow\|Elik'r Stormswallow]]'s confident smirk faltered. "I... well..."

[[NPCs/Co'narr 'Banewatcher' Hyborian\|Co'narr 'Banewatcher' Hyborian]] roared with laughter, slapping the table hard enough to make the tankards jump. "Nice try! But if there's one thing I know, it's what a drunk tankard looks like versus a fresh one!"

He grabbed one of the pristine mugs and raised it high: "To honesty! And to ACTUAL drinking!" He downed it in one go.

[[Party Members/Elik'r Stormswallow\|Elik'r Stormswallow]] couldn't help but laugh at his own terrible deception attempt.

By the time [[Party Members/ToooAh\|ToooAh]] arrived, [[NPCs/Co'narr 'Banewatcher' Hyborian\|Co'narr 'Banewatcher' Hyborian]] had genuinely surrounded himself with **actually** empty tankards, making [[Party Members/Elik'r Stormswallow\|Elik'r Stormswallow]]'s earlier claim look even more pathetic in retrospect.

**Significance:** This provided perfect comic relief after the tension of the challenge. The natural 1 deception roll made the attempted trick hilariously obvious. It showcased [[Party Members/Elik'r Stormswallow\|Elik'r Stormswallow]]'s playful side whilst highlighting [[NPCs/Co'narr 'Banewatcher' Hyborian\|Co'narr 'Banewatcher' Hyborian]]'s inability to be fooled about drinking. The moment also established the lighter atmosphere at the feast as party members returned, contrasting with the serious challenge they'd just survived.

When [[Party Members/ToooAh\|ToooAh]] finally materialised back at the feast, exhausted from solving five rooms of riddles, the scene had transformed. [[NPCs/Co'narr 'Banewatcher' Hyborian\|Co'narr 'Banewatcher' Hyborian]] was surrounded by empty tankards. **Many** empty tankards. Actually empty this time.

"TOOOAH!" [[NPCs/Co'narr 'Banewatcher' Hyborian\|Co'narr 'Banewatcher' Hyborian]] bellowed, raising his current drink. His cheeks were flushed, his grin enormous. "You made it! Brilliant! Here, have a drink!"

[[Party Members/ToooAh\|ToooAh]], still processing the ordeal, accepted. "How long have you been back?"

"Oh, not that long!" [[NPCs/Co'narr 'Banewatcher' Hyborian\|Co'narr 'Banewatcher' Hyborian]] laughed, gesturing at the impressive array of empty vessels. "Third one through! Not bad for an old warrior, eh?" He slapped the table, causing several mugs to jump. "Those riddles were FUN! Especially that one about-" he paused dramatically, "-ALCOHOL!" He roared with laughter.

[[Party Members/ToooAh\|ToooAh]] couldn't help but smile despite his exhaustion. The image of [[NPCs/Co'narr 'Banewatcher' Hyborian\|Co'narr 'Banewatcher' Hyborian]] confidently solving a riddle about drinking while others struggled was perfectly in character.

"That one would've been instant for you," [[Party Members/ToooAh\|ToooAh]] observed.

[[Party Members/Elik'r Stormswallow\|Elik'r Stormswallow]] interjected dryly: "He's been drinking since the moment he got back. I tried to claim I'd drunk these before he arrived, but he saw right through me."

"Course I did!" [[NPCs/Co'narr 'Banewatcher' Hyborian\|Co'narr 'Banewatcher' Hyborian]] said proudly. "Nobody fools me about drinking!"

When [[Party Members/Lady Odonia of Galantri\|Lady Odonia of Galantri]] arrived moments later, [[NPCs/Co'narr 'Banewatcher' Hyborian\|Co'narr 'Banewatcher' Hyborian]] immediately offered her a drink too, his celebration continuing unabated.

**Significance:** This extended the comedy of [[Party Members/Elik'r Stormswallow\|Elik'r Stormswallow]]'s failed deception whilst showcasing [[NPCs/Co'narr 'Banewatcher' Hyborian\|Co'narr 'Banewatcher' Hyborian]]'s genuine celebratory nature. His comment about the alcohol riddle being "instant" for him was both humorous and probably accurate. The contrast between his jovial celebration and the ordeal the others had just endured provided essential comic relief. It also showed [[NPCs/Co'narr 'Banewatcher' Hyborian\|Co'narr 'Banewatcher' Hyborian]]'s genuine pride in the party's collective success.
