## THE HORN INCIDENT (Session 1) - Campaign-Defining Moment

**Context:**

- Party reached underground church/chapel in mountain
- At far end: statue holding a horn (made from animal tusk/horn)
- Party had navigated corridors, defeated two gargoyle statues
- Pursuing dwarf forces hot on heels
- Stone door dropped behind them to halt advance

**Joneyentee's Legendary Overthinking:**

1. **The Simple Solution:** Could have used Mage Hand cantrip to retrieve horn safely
2. **What He Did:** Decided to physically climb the statue instead
3. **Result:** Slipped and fell, took HP damage
4. **The Consequence:** [[Party Members/Ser Pas Rata\|Ser Pas Rata]] then climbed successfully and retrieved the horn

**Pas Rata Blows the Horn:**

- [[Party Members/Ser Pas Rata\|Ser Pas Rata]] proceeded to blow the horn
- **Entire mountain/island began shaking violently**
- Party escaped along different passage
- Emerged on other side of mountain
- **Island erupted as volcano**
- **Massive dragon seen flying away** - This was [[NPCs/The Bane\|The Bane]] being released
- Party believed blowing horn awakened and released The Bane into the world

**Campaign Impact:**

- **THE BANE RELEASED:** Joneyentee's failed climb → Pas Rata blows horn → Bane freed
- **Entire Campaign Crisis:** All subsequent events stem from this moment
- **Corruption Spreading:** Bane now at large, corrupting people and places
- **Civil War:** Bane's influence contributing to kingdom's collapse
- **Party's Burden:** They released the ancient evil (unknowingly)
- **Legendary Campaign Joke:** Joneyentee's overthinking = world-ending consequences

**The Irony:**

- Had Joneyentee just used Mage Hand (his signature spell)...
- Horn might never have been blown
- Bane might still be imprisoned
- Campaign's entire trajectory changed by one bad decision
