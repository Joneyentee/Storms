# Belendar - Palace Ramparts

**Type:** Fortification/Castle Defenses  
**Region:** [[Locations/Belendar\|Belendar]], Palace District  
**Status:** Under Siege (Reinforcements Arrived)  
**Tags:** #location/castle #location/allied #location/under-threat #location/active-quest

### Description

Defensive rampart walls surrounding the royal palace of [[Locations/Belendar\|Belendar]], featuring elevated walkways, archer positions, and tactical defensive structures. The ramparts overlook a long bridge spanning the river, serving as the primary approach to the palace. During Sessions 24-25, these walls became the front line of defense against Falcon Faction forces, with mages maintaining a massive lightning barrier between rampart towers to block siege wyverns. Two of four attacking wyverns were destroyed here before party moved to throne room.

### Geography

Stone fortification walls flanking both sides of palace bridge approach. Walls feature elevated walkways for defenders, with rampart towers at key positions. Bridge spans approximately 150-200 meters over river. Palace square lies beyond bridge, approximately 150 meters from shield wall position. River flows beneath, providing natural defensive barrier. Ramparts approximately 20+ feet high, requiring rope climbing from river level.

### Key Features

- **Lightning Barrier System** - Permanent defensive capability requiring 10+ mages concentrating together on each wall side, blocks siege wyverns temporarily but allows arrows through, damages creatures attempting to pass, lapses create gaps allowing enemy forces through when mages lose concentration
- **Elevated Walkways** - Stone pathways along wall tops providing defensive positions with commanding views
- **Archer Positions** - Designated firing positions, approximately 20+ archers per section
- **Rampart Towers** - Corner/junction towers housing mage concentrations for barrier maintenance
- **Bridge Approach** - Long fortified bridge over river leading to palace gates (primary access route)
- **Wall Access Points** - Rope climbing positions from river level, internal stairs/ladders
