## Description

Defensive rampart walls surrounding the royal palace of [[Locations/Belendar\|Belendar]], featuring elevated walkways, archer positions, and tactical defensive structures. The ramparts overlook a long bridge that spans the river, serving as the primary approach to the palace. During the civil war (Sessions 24-25), these walls became the front line of defense, with mages maintaining a massive lightning barrier between the rampart towers to block advancing siege wyverns. The walls provide commanding views of the palace square, bridge approach, and surrounding city districts.

---

## Geography

**Layout:**

- Stone fortification walls flanking both sides of palace bridge approach
- Elevated walkways for defenders with rampart towers at key positions
- Bridge spans approximately 150-200 meters over river
- Palace square lies beyond bridge, approximately 150 meters from shield wall position
- River flows beneath bridge providing natural defensive barrier
- Ramparts approximately 20+ feet high requiring rope climbing from river level

**Defensive Features:**

- Bulwarks at wall ends providing cover and elevated firing positions
- Multiple archer positions along walkways
- Rampart towers at corners/junctions
- Internal stairs/ladders and external rope access points

## Key Features

- **Lightning Barrier System** - Magical wall spanning between rampart towers, requires 10+ mages concentrating on each wall side, blocks siege wyverns but allows arrows through, damages creatures attempting to pass (radiant/lightning damage), lapses when mages lose concentration
- **Elevated Walkways** - Stone pathways along wall tops providing defensive positions with commanding views
- **Archer Positions** - Designated firing positions, approximately 20+ archers per section
- **Rampart Towers** - Corner/junction towers housing mage concentrations for barrier maintenance
- **Bulwarks** - Defensive stone protrusions at wall ends providing cover and elevated firing positions
- **Bridge Approach** - Long fortified bridge over river leading to palace gates (primary access route)
- **Wall Access Points** - Rope climbing positions from river level, internal stairs/ladders
- **Ballistae/Siege Equipment** - Defensive artillery positions (implied but not detailed)
